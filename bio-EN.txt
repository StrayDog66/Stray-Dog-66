LONG BIO (250 words) — FOR PRESS KIT, INTERVIEWS, BOOKING
═══════════════════════════════════════════════

Stray Dog —Perro Callejero— is the alias of a producer
and DJ born in Asunción, Paraguay, and based in Panama
City. His path reflects two geographies and two sensibilities:
the thick melancholy of the Paraguay River and the electric
pulse of the Panamanian tropics. It's that contrast — the
southern nostalgia with the Caribbean humidity — that
shapes a sound rejecting polish in favor of rawness, and
samples in favor of real machines.

In 2026 he founds 66 RECORDS, a label dedicated to raw
techno and dark house from Latin America. His debut
"Feral Frequency" (SXD-066) opens the catalog with four
tracks recorded between Asunción and Panama, without
sample-packs or quantized drums. The limited edition of
666 hand-numbered vinyls is conceived as a collectible:
each copy is unique, each number deliberate.

His schedule includes two recurring radio shows:
THE GROWLER, a bi-weekly Thursday session where he shares
deep music with a beer in hand, and OFF LEASH, the weekly
Friday warm-up for those heading out to the dancefloor.

Live, Stray Dog combines modular hardware, Roland machines,
and a brutalist visual system he designs himself. His
philosophy isn't complicated: no leash, no master, no
mercy — but with good musical taste.
